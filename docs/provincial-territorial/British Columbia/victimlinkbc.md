## About
VictimLinkBC is a **toll-free, confidential** support program for people affected by **crime and trauma**. It provides information and referrals to community, social, health, justice, and government resources, and offers **immediate crisis and emotional support** for victims of family and sexual violence (including human trafficking).

## Coverage
- **British Columbia and Yukon**

## Who it’s for
- People who have experienced (or aren’t sure if they have experienced) **crime or trauma**
- Victims/survivors of **family or sexual violence**, including **human trafficking**

## Contact
- **Call or text (toll-free):** 1-800-563-0808
- **Email:** 211-victimlinkbc@uwbc.ca
- **Website:** https://victimlinkbc.ca/

## Hours
- **24/7/365**

## Languages
- VictimLinkBC states its phone service is available in **240 languages and dialects** (including many North American Indigenous languages), with interpretation support available **24/7**.

## What to expect when you call or text
VictimLinkBC can help by:
- Connecting you to services that fit your needs (e.g., community-based victim services, transition houses, counselling resources)
- Sharing information about the justice system, safety planning, and other supports/resources

## Privacy and safety approach
- The service is described as **confidential**.
- The website includes a **“Safety Exit”** option to leave the site quickly.
