## About
**NWT 811** is a **free, confidential, 24/7** phone line where you can speak with a **registered nurse** for health advice, including **mental wellness and addiction recovery** support. You can ask to **stay anonymous**.

## Coverage
- **Northwest Territories (Canada)**

## Who it’s for
- Anyone in the NWT who needs non-emergency health advice
- Anyone looking for support with **stress, anxiety, depression, suicidal thoughts, grief/loss, abuse, sexual assault, mood changes, or substance use**
- People seeking help to **quit smoking or vaping**
- People with questions about someone they care about

## Contact
- **Dial:** 811  
- **Toll-free (if 811 doesn’t connect):** 1-844-259-1793  
- **Website:** https://www.hss.gov.nt.ca/en/services/811

## Hours
- **24/7/365**

## Languages
- **English and French**
- **Translation services for all official NWT languages** (including Indigenous languages) — ask when you connect

## What to expect when you call or text
- You will speak with a registered nurse who can assess your situation, provide advice, and connect you to supports in your region.
- For the **Mental Health and Wellness Support line**, you may be prompted to **press 1**.
- Follow-up support may be offered.

## Notes
- If you can’t connect to **811**, call **1-844-259-1793** instead. If you’re calling from a cell phone, turning off **Wi‑Fi Calling** may help.
- You can also use **811** to reach **poison control**.
