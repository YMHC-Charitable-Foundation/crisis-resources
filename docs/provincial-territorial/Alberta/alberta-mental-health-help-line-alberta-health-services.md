## About
The Alberta Mental Health Help Line is a confidential, 24/7 service that provides support, information, and referrals to Albertans experiencing mental health concerns. The line is staffed by a multidisciplinary team of health professionals, including nurses, psychiatric nurses, social workers, occupational therapists, and psychologists.

## Coverage
**Alberta-wide**

## Who it’s for
- Albertans of all ages experiencing mental health concerns
- Individuals looking for information or referrals for themselves or others

## Contact
- **Call:** 1-877-303-2642 (toll-free)
- **Health Link:** Dial 811 and ask for the Mental Health Help Line

## Hours
- **24/7/365**

## Languages
- **English**
- Interpretation services are available in over **170 languages** (ask for an interpreter when you call)

## What to expect when you call
You will speak directly with a trained mental health professional. Services provided include:
- Crisis support
- Mental health screening and assessment over the phone
- General information about mental health topics
- Referrals to local services and information on how to access them
- Strategies to support emotional well-being at home

## Privacy and safety approach
- The service is **confidential**.
- Calls are answered by registered professionals within Alberta Health Services.

## Notes
- If you are experiencing addiction concerns, you can also call the **Addiction Helpline** at 1-866-332-2322 (available 24/7).
