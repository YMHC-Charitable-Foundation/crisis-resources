## About

The Saskatoon Mental Health & Addictions Central Intake is the point of entry for non‑emergency mental health and addiction services provided through the Saskatchewan Health Authority in Saskatoon. Intake workers assess needs and help connect callers to appropriate counselling, treatment programs and community resources.

## Coverage

**Saskatoon and surrounding area**, Saskatchewan, Canada.

## Who it’s for

- Adults, youth and families seeking assessment or referral for mental health or addictions services in the Saskatoon area.
- People experiencing non‑urgent mental health or substance use concerns.
- Anyone looking for information or a referral to community resources.

## Contact

- **Phone (Central Intake Line):** 306‑655‑7777
- **Address:** 715 Queen Street, Saskatoon, SK S7K 4X4 (Sturdy Stone Centre). Intake is offered by phone; in‑person visits generally require an appointment.

## Hours

- **Monday–Friday:** 8:00 am – 12:00 pm and 12:30 pm – 4:30 pm
- **Closed:** Evenings, weekends and statutory holidays

## Languages

- **English** (interpreter services for other languages may be available on request).

## What to expect when you call

When you call, an intake worker will ask about your mental health or addiction concerns and collect basic information. They will assess your needs and connect you to appropriate Saskatchewan Health Authority programs, community‑based counselling or other supports. Calls may involve wait times if the line is busy.

## Notes

- If you need after‑hours or emergency help, call **9‑8‑8** (national suicide and mental health crisis line), **HealthLine 811** or **911** if there is an immediate risk.
- People outside Saskatoon or in the Five Hills Health Region should call **306‑691‑6464** or **1‑877‑564‑0543** for mental health and addictions intake.
- HealthLine 811 is a confidential 24/7 health and mental health advice line staffed by registered nurses and social workers.
