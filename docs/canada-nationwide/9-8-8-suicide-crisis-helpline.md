---

## About
9-8-8 is Canada’s national suicide crisis helpline, providing a safe, judgment-free space to talk if you are thinking about suicide or worried about someone else.

## Who it’s for
- Anyone in Canada who is thinking about suicide, or concerned about someone else
- People who want support in English or French (with interpreter support potentially available by phone)

## Contact
- **Call:** 9-8-8 (toll-free)
- **Text:** 9-8-8

## Hours
- **24/7/365**

## Languages
- **English and French** (you can choose at the start of a call/text)
- Interpreter support for other languages may be available by phone — ask when you connect

## What to expect when you call or text
A 9-8-8 responder will listen without judgment, engage with empathy, and support you in exploring ways to create safety when things feel overwhelming.

At the start of a call, you may:
- Hear a brief recorded message
- Choose **English or French**
- In most cases, choose to indicate if you are **under 18** or **First Nations, Inuit, or Métis**

In some parts of Canada, calls/texts are connected to the existing crisis service in your province/territory.

## Accessibility
- Canada VRS (ASL/LSQ via video interpreter)
- TTY / IP Relay Services (dial **7-1-1** for TTY relay)

## Cost
- Calling is toll-free.
- Texting is free if you have unlimited texting; standard carrier messaging rates may apply if you exceed your plan.
